Museum of Non-Existent Things

A quiet threshold between what we imagine and what imagines us back.

Whispered online as a “character that isn’t a character,” duang began as a sound, then became a sign—an internet echo often described as a non-existent Chinese character. It has no fixed strokes, yet it travels; no official body, yet it gathers meaning. This museum follows that ripple: a gallery of myths, concepts, blueprints, and digital phantoms that move people without ever taking a solid form. Step carefully—some absences feel almost real.
